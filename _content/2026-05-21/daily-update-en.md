## SECTION 1 — GLOBAL EVENTS

**Dow holds 49,482 as S&P 500 and Nasdaq post fresh all-time highs Thursday**
Equities showed a split personality Wednesday into Thursday, with the Dow Jones Industrial Average holding its Tuesday close of 49,482.90 while the S&P 500 and Nasdaq each struck new record highs on the same session — suggesting large-cap tech is carrying the market while rate-sensitive industrials lag. **April CPI locked at 3.8% annualized — Fed dissent at highest since 1992**
The Bureau of Labor Statistics confirmed April 2026 CPI at 3.8% year-over-year, well above the Fed's 2% target and driven by energy and food price acceleration tied to the ongoing Hormuz supply disruption. The May FOMC meeting ended with four dissents — the most in a single meeting since 1992 — as Governor Stephen Miran pushed for a 25 bp cut while three regional presidents objected to language markets interpreted as dovish signaling. Cleveland Fed nowcasting places May 2026 CPI at 4.18% annualized, with only five of thirty tracked categories running at or below target, reinforcing the view that rate relief is off the table through at least Q3. *(CNBC / Cleveland Fed / WA Trust, May 12–15, 2026)*

**WTI near $103.8 and Brent above $107 as Hormuz losses escalate and IEA warns on inventory depletion**
WTI crude held near $103.83 per barrel and Brent remained above $107 Thursday as the IEA flagged that losses in the Strait of Hormuz are escalating and contributing to a record depletion of global oil inventories. OPEC's April production fell 1.7 million barrels per day — a cumulative decline of over 30% since the outbreak of the Iran conflict — with the UAE's formal exit from the organization on May 1 removing one of the cartel's largest swing producers from collective coordination. OPEC simultaneously trimmed its 2026 global demand growth forecast to 1.17 million bpd, down from 1.38 million bpd, adding a demand-side complication to the already strained supply picture. *(Gurufocus / OPEC / Petra, May 13–21, 2026)*

**Gold slides toward $4,350 as dollar decline fails to provide floor amid rising real yields**
Gold retreated to approximately $4,350 Thursday, consistent with CoinCodex model projections of $4,349.90 for May 21, continuing a pullback that has now seen the metal shed roughly 16% from its January all-time high of $5,589. The DXY index is declining, which would ordinarily support gold, but 10-year Treasury yields rising to approximately 4.52% — near the highest level since May 2025 — are increasing the opportunity cost of holding non-yielding assets. Spot gold touched $4,619.61 during the week of May 15, its lowest level since May 6, as institutional rotation toward cash-generating equities and US dollar assets accelerated on the back of stronger real yields. *(CoinCodex / Crux Investor / GoldSilver.com, May 15–21, 2026)*

**World Bank flags global growth slowdown in 2026 as trade barriers and policy uncertainty compound energy shock**
The World Bank's Global Economic Prospects report projects global growth edging lower in 2026, with the slowdown attributed to rising trade barriers, elevated policy uncertainty, and the cascading macro effects of the Middle East conflict. US growth is running slightly above 2%, but the fiscal deficit is projected at a minimum of 5.5% of GDP — a structural imbalance that limits the government's capacity to cushion any demand shock from sustained high energy prices. Western European inflation is expected to converge back toward target while US inflation remains sticky near 3–4%, widening the transatlantic monetary policy gap and complicating coordinated global responses. *(World Bank / Atlantic Council / Deloitte, 2026)*
