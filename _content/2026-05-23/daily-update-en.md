Brent crude settled near $105.75 and WTI at $98.00, pulling back from the $109 and $102 levels seen in mid-May when Strait of Hormuz restrictions were at their most acute. Analysts at multiple institutions continue to frame the drawdown as a consolidation within a secular bull trend rather than a structural reversal. *(Trading Economics; Investing.com; May 22–23, 2026)*

**IMF flags Middle East conflict as primary 2026 growth risk; global expansion slows to 3.1%**
The IMF's April 2026 World Economic Outlook projects global growth slowing to 3.1% in 2026 from a prior baseline, with the outbreak of conflict in the Middle East identified as the decisive new downside variable on top of already-elevated trade barriers. Global headline inflation is projected to rise modestly in 2026 before resuming its decline in 2027, with the slowdown in growth and the inflation pickup expected to be "particularly pronounced" in energy-importing emerging market economies. Morgan Stanley's regional analysis highlights Europe as most exposed to the energy shock given faster passthrough of oil prices into household bills, while China's pre-built stockpiles offer near-term insulation. *(IMF World Economic Outlook, April 2026; Morgan Stanley 2026 Outlook)*
