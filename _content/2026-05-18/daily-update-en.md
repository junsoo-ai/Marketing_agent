## SECTION 1 — GLOBAL EVENTS

**Warsh Takes Fed Chair; 30-Year Treasury Yield Clears 5%**
Kevin Warsh was confirmed by the Senate and formally succeeded Jerome Powell as Federal Reserve Chairman, with the 30-year US Treasury yield hitting 5.02% — its highest level since 2007 — while the 10-year settled at 4.44%. Bond markets are pricing out near-term rate cuts as Warsh inherits a sticky inflation picture compounded by energy-driven price pressure from ongoing Middle East tensions. *(Euronews, May 14, 2026)*

**US CPI Rose 3.8% in April, Above the 3.7% Consensus**
US consumer prices climbed 3.8% year-over-year in April 2026, above the 3.7% forecast, with energy prices up nearly 18% annually as oil supply disruptions from the Iran conflict filtered through to household costs. The hotter-than-expected print eliminates the near-term case for a rate cut and reinforces the bond sell-off that sent 30-year yields above 5% for the first time in almost two decades. *(Fortune, May 2026)*

**S&P 500 Fell 1.24% on Friday; Dow Shed 537 Points**
The S&P 500 closed at 7,408.50 (-1.24%) on May 15 as the Dow Jones fell 537 points to 49,526 and the Nasdaq Composite dropped 1.54% to 26,225, with technology stocks leading the selloff as Treasury yields spiked on the Warsh confirmation. A US-China summit ended without meaningful trade policy outcomes, adding a second layer of risk-off pressure to what was already a yield-driven session. *(CNBC, May 15, 2026)*

**RBA Raised Rates to 4.35% — Third Consecutive 2026 Hike**
The Reserve Bank of Australia lifted its cash rate 25 basis points to 4.35% in an 8-1 vote on May 5, as Middle East-driven energy costs pushed the revised headline inflation peak to 4.8% by mid-2026. The RBA signaled scope to pause, though Westpac is forecasting two additional 25bp hikes by August that would bring the rate to 4.85%. *(RBA / Canstar, May 5, 2026)*

**IEA: Global Oil Demand to Contract 420 kb/d in 2026 as Geopolitical Risk Elevates Prices**
The IEA's May Oil Market Report forecasts world oil demand contracting 420 kb/d year-over-year in 2026, with the sharpest quarterly decline of 2.45 mb/d arriving in Q2. Despite weak fundamentals, Brent crude reached $109.26 per barrel by May 15 as Strait of Hormuz supply concerns overrode the bearish demand picture — creating a price-fundamental divergence that is complicating inflation management across multiple central banks. *(IEA Oil Market Report / Fortune, May 2026)*
