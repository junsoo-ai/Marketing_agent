## SECTION 1 — GLOBAL EVENTS

**WTI crude holds above $102 as Hormuz disruption fears persist**
WTI crude closed at $102.84 on May 19, with Brent at $110.92, keeping oil comfortably above the $100 threshold that has rattled global input-cost outlooks. **UAE exits OPEC, reshaping global energy governance**
The United Arab Emirates formally departed OPEC in late April 2026 after nearly six decades of membership, citing a desire to maximize production capacity and respond independently to global demand signals amid the Iran conflict. The exit restructures the bloc's internal production calculus and removes one of its historically largest quota holders. Analysts note the move further complicates OPEC+'s ability to coordinate supply restraint at a time when geopolitical disruption is already lifting prices. *(Renewable Matter, April 29, 2026)*

**Cleveland Fed nowcast pegs May CPI at 4.18% annualised**
The Federal Reserve Bank of Cleveland's inflation nowcast projects May 2026 CPI at a 0.46% month-over-month change, translating to approximately 4.18% on an annualised basis, with core CPI at 0.23% MoM (roughly 2.82% annualised). The reading sits well above the Fed's 2% annual target and arrives alongside a 5-year expected inflation figure of 2.48% recorded in April — a metric watched closely for longer-term anchoring signals. Only 5 of 30 tracked CPI sub-categories are running at or below the 2% target, with 10 categories registering above 5% year-over-year. *(Cleveland Fed Inflation Nowcasting, updated May 15, 2026; WA Trust Economic Perspectives, May 15, 2026)*

**Global growth outlook darkens as trade barriers and energy shock bite**
The World Bank's latest Global Economic Prospects report warns that global growth is set to edge lower in 2026, with the slowdown attributed to rising trade barriers and elevated policy uncertainty. Morgan Stanley's regional analysis highlights Europe as the most exposed major economy to the current energy shock, given faster energy passthrough into household bills, while China remains comparatively insulated due to prior strategic stockpiling. The IEA forecasts a potential oversupply of 3.7 million barrels per day if geopolitical tensions ease — a scenario that could sharply reprice energy markets. *(World Bank Global Economic Prospects, 2026; Morgan Stanley 2026 Outlooks)*
