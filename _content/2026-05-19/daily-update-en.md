## SECTION 1 — GLOBAL EVENTS

**Inflation now projected to hit 6% by Q2 — Fed has no room to move**
A CNBC survey of the nation's top economic forecasters published Friday put consumer price inflation on track to reach 6% in the second quarter of 2026, the most aggressive projection since the post-COVID cycle peaked. Energy prices are up 18% year-over-year and food costs are accelerating, with the April CPI print of 3.8% already above the 3.7% consensus. The Federal Reserve, now under Chair Warsh, is holding rates unchanged while monitoring conditions — but with a 6% trajectory incoming, rate cuts are firmly off the table for the foreseeable future. *(CNBC, May 15, 2026)*

**Brent crude crosses $111 as Hormuz closure and OPEC cuts collide**
Brent oil surged to $111.38 per barrel Tuesday, up 1.94% on the session, while WTI climbed to $103.47 (+2.43%), as markets continued to price geopolitical risk premium from the closed Strait of Hormuz into the forward curve. OPEC's May 13 monthly report had already cut its 2026 global oil demand growth forecast to an average of 104.57 million barrels per day for Q2 — down 500,000 bpd from the prior estimate — yet the supply disruption narrative is overwhelmingly overriding the demand-side revision. Peace talks between the US and Iran remain stalled, and there is no credible timeline for Hormuz reopening. *(GuruFocus / Investing.com, May 13–19, 2026)*

**Global bond markets under pressure as 30-year yields reach generational highs**
Longer-duration debt continued to sell off across major economies, with the 30-year UK gilt yield closing at 5.77% — its highest level since 1998 — driven by a combination of global energy price pressures and deteriorating UK growth expectations. The US 10-year Treasury yield rose to 4.623% on Tuesday (+0.024%), while the 30-year sits at 5.145%. ATB Financial's weekly market note flagged that investors are now demanding a meaningful term premium on long-dated bonds as geopolitical and inflationary uncertainty refuses to abate. The VIX edged higher to 18.43, up 6.78% on the day, reflecting sustained unease across risk assets. *(ATB Financial / Investing.com, May 19, 2026)*

**Alibaba rallies 8.2% despite 84% collapse in core profitability**
US-listed shares of Alibaba surged 8.2% on Tuesday as investors rewarded the company's aggressive technology spending and AI infrastructure build-out, choosing to look through an 84% plunge in core profitability. The move illustrates a bifurcated market sentiment in which forward-looking capital deployment into AI is being valued above near-term earnings, even as macro headwinds from US-China trade uncertainty persist. The rally also provided a modest tailwind to broader Chinese tech exposure at a time when most global equities were under pressure from the bond market and energy price shock. *(ATB Financial, May 19, 2026)*

**Central banks hold but warn — Warsh Fed and global peers in monitoring mode**
Following the RBA's third consecutive 2026 rate hike to 4.35% last week, major central banks including the Fed, ECB, and Bank of Canada are characterised this month as "holding rates unchanged but monitoring closely" as WTW's Global Markets Overview described the consensus stance. FOMC minutes are due this week and will be closely parsed for any signal that the Warsh-led Fed is prepared to resume hiking if the 6% inflation projection materialises. The Trading Week Ahead note from ThinkCapital identified the FOMC release as a potential inflection point for both rates and FX positioning, particularly against a backdrop of a rising Dollar Index at 99.31. *(WTW / ThinkCapital, May 14–19, 2026)*
