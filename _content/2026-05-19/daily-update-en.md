## SECTION 1 — GLOBAL EVENTS

**US equities extend losses as yield pressure and macro headwinds compound into a second consecutive red session**
The S&P 500 closed at 7,408.50 (−1.24%), the Nasdaq fell to 26,225.15 (−1.54%), and the Dow settled at 49,526.17 (−1.07%) on Monday. Risk-off flows accelerated as new Fed chair Kevin Warsh's hawkish signals reinforced expectations that rate cuts remain firmly off the table for 2026, while the Dollar Index firmed to 99.21. The VIX spiked 6.78% to 18.43, reflecting a meaningful uptick in near-term hedging demand across equity desks. *(Investing.com, Yahoo Finance, May 19 2026)*

**WTI crude surges past $107 as Hormuz supply disruption overwhelms OPEC demand downgrade**
WTI crude jumped to $107.72 (+2.18%) while Brent held in the $109–$110 range, extending a weekly gain that analysts estimate at approximately 11% through the week ending May 16. The rally continues despite OPEC trimming its 2026 global oil demand growth forecast to 1.17 million barrels per day — down from 1.38 million bpd — citing geopolitical disruption and tariff drag. The effective closure of the Strait of Hormuz has removed millions of barrels of Middle East output from the global supply chain, overriding the fundamentally weaker demand signal. *(Reuters, OPEC Monthly Report, Univest, May 13–19 2026)*

**April US CPI holds at 3.8% as energy costs embed inflation above Fed target**
The Bureau of Labor Statistics confirmed April 2026 CPI at 3.8% year-over-year, with energy prices up approximately 18% annually and core CPI at 2.8%. The Federal Reserve's May meeting saw four dissents — the highest since 1992 — with Governor Miran voting for a 25 bp cut while three regional presidents objected to accommodative language. The New York Fed's April Survey of Consumer Expectations recorded a further rise in short-term inflation expectations, underscoring that the transmission of energy and tariff shocks into household psychology remains active. *(BLS, CNBC, New York Fed, May 7–12 2026)*

**Gold corrects sharply as dollar strengthens and risk-off flows rotate to cash**
Gold futures fell 3.02% to $4,543.60 on Monday, unwinding a portion of its record-high run as the stronger DXY (99.21) and rising real yields pressured the metal. The ATB Financial weekly market update noted that the TSX Composite closed lower in the prior week primarily because of a sharp drawdown in the materials sector, driven by precious metals selling. The correction follows a period in which gold had benefited from Hormuz-linked geopolitical risk premia, suggesting some normalisation of that premium is now underway even as underlying supply uncertainty persists. *(MarketWatch, ATB Financial, May 19 2026)*

**Global growth forecast edges lower as trade barriers and FOMC uncertainty widen policy fog**
The World Bank's latest Global Economic Prospects report projects global growth to edge down in 2026, partly reflecting the cumulative drag from elevated trade barriers and persistent policy uncertainty. FOMC minutes are due on May 19, adding a near-term catalyst risk: analysts at Univest flagged the release as a potential headwind for crude and broader risk assets given the market's sensitivity to any language shift on the rate path. Deloitte's global outlook and WTW's May markets overview both highlight that gains in the S&P 500 remain heavily concentrated in a small cohort of AI-related technology names, leaving index-level resilience shallower than headline figures suggest. *(World Bank, Deloitte, WTW, Univest, May 2026)*
