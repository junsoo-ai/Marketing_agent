## SECTION 1 — GLOBAL EVENTS

**Dow edges higher Monday but S&P slips as mixed session follows Friday's rout**
The Dow Jones Industrial Average added 160 points (+0.32%) to close at 49,686 on Monday, led by 3M (+4.32%), Salesforce (+3.38%), and Chevron (+2.57%). The S&P 500 finished fractionally lower at 7,403, down 0.15%, as Caterpillar (-2.90%), Nvidia (-1.39%), and UnitedHealth (-0.85%) weighed on the index. The divergence between Dow and S&P signals a rotation toward value and industrial names away from growth and tech as yield pressure persists. *(Trading Economics, May 19, 2026)*

**Gold surges past $4,830 as safe-haven demand intensifies amid US-Iran oil uncertainty**
Spot gold climbed to $4,830.92 on Tuesday, a sharp recovery from the $4,540 weekly close on May 15 — a level that represented a 3.7% weekly decline. The rebound tracks renewed geopolitical anxiety in the Middle East, with Bloomberg reporting that Asian markets are watching oil swings closely as US-Iran negotiations remain unresolved and Secretary Bessent indicated no near-term normalization of oil markets. A 30-day sanctions waiver on stranded Russian oil has added further ambiguity to energy pricing. *(150currency.com, Bloomberg Asia Trade, May 19, 2026)*

**WTI crude holds above $103 as Iran ceasefire talks stall and OPEC output remains constrained**
WTI crude settled at $103.09 on May 19, continuing its elevated trajectory following Trump's May 10 declaration that an Iran ceasefire was "on life support." OPEC crude output fell to its lowest level in more than two decades in April, contracting by approximately 830,000 barrels per day as the effective closure of the Strait of Hormuz disrupted export flows. Saudi crude nominations to China are expected to fall further in June as buyers respond to elevated conflict-linked pricing. *(Reuters, May 10–19, 2026)*

**UK 30-year gilt yield closes at highest level since 1998 amid global bond market pressure**
The 30-year UK government bond yield hit a new closing high of 5.77% this week, its loftiest level since 1998, driven by global energy price pressures and sustained concerns over the UK's fiscal trajectory. The move reflects a broader investor demand for a greater premium on long-duration sovereign debt amid geopolitical and economic uncertainty. The development echoes the post-Warsh confirmation spike in US 30-year yields that cleared 5% on Friday, suggesting a synchronized global repricing of long-end risk. *(ATB Financial Weekly Market Update, May 19, 2026)*

**Alibaba US-listed shares rally 8.2% as investors reward AI investment despite profit collapse**
US-listed shares of Alibaba surged 8.2% in Monday's session even as the company reported an 84% plunge in core profitability. Investors chose to overlook the earnings collapse and instead rewarded aggressive technology spending commitments, particularly in artificial intelligence infrastructure. The move is consistent with a broader 2026 equity market pattern in which AI-aligned capital expenditure signals are being priced as future-value catalysts regardless of near-term profitability. *(ATB Financial Weekly Market Update, May 19, 2026)*
