**Global equity markets retreat as bond yields surge on inflation fears**
U.S. indices closed sharply lower on May 26, with the S&P 500 settling at 7,403.05 (−0.07%), the Dow Jones Industrial Average sliding 1.07% to 49,526.17, and the Nasdaq giving back 1.54% to finish at 26,225.14. The selloff was attributed to renewed anxiety over sticky inflation and rising Treasury yields that compressed risk appetite across the board. The 10-year yield held at 4.572%, reinforcing the "higher-for-longer" narrative ahead of the next Federal Reserve meeting. *(Barchart / Facebook–Texas News Sports, May 26 2026)*

**April CPI confirms inflation stuck well above Fed's 2% target**
The Bureau of Labor Statistics confirmed April 2026 headline CPI at 3.8% year-over-year, the highest annual print since May 2023 and 50 basis points above March's reading. Core CPI (ex-food and energy) rose 0.4% month-over-month and 2.8% annually, with the monthly rate marking its steepest climb since January 2025. Energy prices jumped 3.8% and accounted for more than 40% of the headline increase, keeping pressure on the Fed to maintain its current restrictive stance. *(BLS CPI Summary, CNBC, May 12 2026)*

**Goldman Sachs projects 2.8% global growth in 2026, led by resilient U.S. economy**
Goldman Sachs Research raised its global GDP growth forecast to 2.8% for 2026, exceeding the consensus estimate of 2.5%, with the United States expected to outperform at 2.6% versus a consensus 2.0%. The outperformance is attributed to reduced tariff drag, enacted tax cuts, and easing financial conditions that have buoyed consumer and corporate confidence. The firm's equity strategists forecast global stocks to return approximately 11% over the next 12 months on the back of sturdy earnings growth. *(Goldman Sachs 2026 Macro Outlook, May 2026)*

**Brent crude retreats sharply as oversupply concerns outweigh geopolitical risk premium**
Brent crude settled at $100.21 on May 25, down markedly from $103.54 the prior Friday and well off the intra-week high of $109.31 reached on May 21, reflecting a rapid deflation of the geopolitical risk premium. WTI held at $96.60, a level consistent with the multi-week downtrend from the $108.66 print on May 18. Analysts note that OPEC+'s share of global supply has declined from roughly 53% in 2016 to approximately 46% in 2025–2026, limiting the cartel's ability to engineer sustained price rallies without ceding market share. *(Yahoo Finance / Investing.com / Equiti Research, May 25 2026)*

**J.P. Morgan warns of persistent global core inflation at 2.8% through 2026**
J.P. Morgan Global Research expects global core CPI to remain stable at 2.8% through 2026, with the United States diverging higher while European inflation moderates toward target. The firm highlights that inflation dynamics are increasingly driven by local labor market tightness rather than common global supply shocks, complicating synchronized central bank easing. The 5-Year, 5-Year Forward Inflation Expectation Rate continues to signal that markets are not fully pricing in a return to the Fed's 2% anchor on any near-term horizon. *(J.P. Morgan Global Research, Feb 17 2026; FRED T5YIFR, May 2026)*
