## SECTION 1 — GLOBAL EVENTS

**S&P 500 and Nasdaq extend record-setting streak on Dell-led AI surge**
US equities pushed higher Friday, with the S&P 500 rising 0.12% to close at approximately 7,520 and the Nasdaq gaining 0.11%, extending what is now a nine-consecutive-week winning streak — the longest since 2023. Dell surged 33% after reporting blowout AI server demand, anchoring a broad rally in AI-linked hardware names. The Dow Jones Industrial Average closed at 51,028, adding roughly 182 points on the session. *(Times of India / AP, May 30, 2026)*

**April PCE inflation hits highest level since 2023, complicating Fed path**
The Federal Reserve's preferred inflation gauge, the PCE index, printed 3.8% headline and 3.3% core year-over-year for April — the highest readings since 2023 and nearly two full percentage points above the Fed's 2% target. Inflation-adjusted consumer spending rose only 0.1% as war-driven energy costs eroded real incomes, while first-quarter GDP contracted, adding a stagflationary undertone to the macro picture. New Fed Chairman Kevin Warsh faces sharply constrained room to maneuver heading into the summer policy calendar. *(Gramercy EM Weekly, May 30, 2026; CNBC, May 12, 2026)*

**US–Iran ceasefire extension talks ease Hormuz supply premium**
Investor sentiment received a meaningful lift Friday as reports emerged that the United States and Iran are actively negotiating an extension of the existing ceasefire arrangement, helping temper fears of a prolonged closure of the Strait of Hormuz. Brent crude pulled back to $91.17/barrel as the geopolitical risk premium built into energy markets during May partially unwound. European and Asian equity markets also traded mostly higher in sympathy, reinforcing the broad risk-on tone that has characterized the final week of May. *(Times of India, May 30, 2026; Trading Economics, May 29, 2026)*

**Gold holds above $4,569 as monthly loss looms despite year-long rally**
Gold settled near $4,569.93/oz on Friday, up 1.66% on the session, but the precious metal remains on track for a roughly 1.15% monthly decline in May — its first meaningful pullback after a dramatic run that saw prices reach an all-time high of $5,608 in January 2026. The partial easing of Middle East tensions reduced safe-haven demand at the margin, even as the metal remains 38.93% higher year-over-year. UBS recently trimmed its 2026 gold price forecast to $5,500/oz, citing markets "rediscovering the concept of opportunity cost" as real yields stay elevated. *(Trading Economics, May 29, 2026; UBS via Kitco, May 27, 2026)*

**OPEC trims 2026 demand growth forecast as UAE exit reshapes cartel dynamics**
OPEC cut its 2026 global oil demand growth forecast to 1.17 million barrels per day, down from 1.38 million bpd previously, citing the economic drag from the Middle East conflict and its disruption to Hormuz transit flows. The revision compounds structural uncertainty already introduced by the UAE's formal exit from both OPEC and OPEC+ effective May 1, 2026 — a historic shift that removes one of the bloc's highest-capacity members and weakens its collective pricing discipline. OPEC raised its 2027 demand growth projection to 1.54 million bpd, signaling confidence in a post-conflict rebound. *(Reuters, May 13, 2026; Gulf News / Facebook, April 28, 2026)*
